To truly master these wired communication protocols, we need to look at them through the **6Ws lens: Who, What, Where, When, Why, and How (the "W" hidden in How)**.

Here is a comprehensive, deep-dive framework organized by topics and subtopics to help you learn and compare SPI, I2C, CAN, UART, USART, and USB.

## How to use this framework for deep learning:

1. **The Physical Layer vs. Data Link Layer:** Notice **Why** CAN and USB are so complex compared to SPI/UART. CAN and USB define both the *physical* characteristics (differential signaling for noise immunity) and the *software protocol layer* (complex packets). SPI and UART are mostly just raw bit-shifters.
2. **Synchronous vs. Asynchronous:** Look at the **When** and **What** questions. Synchronous protocols (SPI, I2C) use a physical clock line to tell the receiver *exactly* when to read the data. Asynchronous protocols (UART, CAN) don't have a clock line; they rely on pre-agreed speeds (baud rates) or internal clock recovery to sample data at the right time.
